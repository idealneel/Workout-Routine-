General Training Principles

Core philosophies underpinning this beginner workout block.

RPE & EFFORT
Aim for RPE 6–8. You should finish each set feeling like you had 2–4 reps left in the tank. Never train to failure as a beginner.
FORM FIRST
Never increase weight until your form is solid. Bad reps with heavy weight build bad habits and cause injury.
PROGRESSIVE OVERLOAD
Add 2.5–5 kg to a lift once you can complete all sets and reps cleanly at RPE 7 or lower.
REST DAYS MATTER
Train Mon / Wed / Fri or any 3 non-consecutive days. Muscle is built during recovery, not just during training.
REST BETWEEN SETS
Rest 2–3 minutes between compound lifts. 60–90 seconds between isolation exercises.
STICK TO THE PLAN
Run this program for at least 12 weeks before changing anything. Consistency beats complexity every time.
FINISHERS
Each day ends with a 10-minute finisher targeting neglected muscles — forearms, traps, and obliques. Small muscles respond fast and prevent imbalances early.
WORKPLACE EFFICIENCY
Short on time? Superset your accessories (e.g., Lateral Raises with Tricep Extensions) to keep the intensity high and the session under 50 minutes.

Warm-up 5 minutes light cardio + shoulder dislocates + scapular circles.

Mag Grip Lat Pulldown

Close Grip Rowing
3×8–12

Keep elbows tucked in to bias the lats. This can be done on an incline bench if machines are occupied.

04
Reverse Flys
3×8–12
Form Tip
Keep your elbows high and pull back with your upper back. This is the key to thick traps and stable shoulders.

05
Dumbbell Shrugs
3×8–12
Form Tip
Lean forward 15 degrees to align the weight with the trap fibers. Avoid rolling your shoulders; pull straight up and slightly back.
Last set: Train to near failure.
06
Bayesian Curls (Cable)
3×8–12
Form Tip
Keep your elbow fixed in place behind your body; do not let it swing forward during the curl.
Last set: Train to near failure.

Dumbbell Hammer Curls
3×8–12
Form Tip

Last set: Train to near failure.

3×8–12

12-WEEK PROGRESSION

A week-by-week roadmap for your first 12 weeks. Follow this exactly before making any changes to the program.

HOW TO USE THIS SECTION

Read through all 4 phases before you start training. You are currently in Phase 1. Mark each week as complete using the checkbox — your progress saves automatically and persists when you close the browser.

1
Weeks 1–3
FOUNDATION

Use light weights only. Focus entirely on learning each movement. End every set with 3–4 reps still in the tank (RPE 5–6). Do not progress weight yet.

2
Weeks 4–6
BUILD

Increase weight slightly if form is solid. Target RPE 6–7. Add 2.5 kg to lower body lifts and 1.25–2.5 kg to upper body lifts when you complete all reps cleanly for 2 sessions in a row.

3
Weeks 7–9
LOAD

Now push to RPE 7–8 on your working sets. Continue adding weight using the same rule. You should be noticeably stronger than Week 1. Track every session.

4
Weeks 10–12
CONSOLIDATE

Maintain intensity. Audit your form on every lift. At Week 12, compare your weights to Week 1 — most beginners see 20–40% strength increases. Then decide: repeat the block with heavier starting weights or move to an intermediate program.

REST DAY ROUTINE

Rest days are not wasted days. Use them to recover smarter, move better and show up stronger tomorrow.

HOW TO USE THIS SECTION

On any rest day, work through the Morning, Mobility and Evening sections in order. You do not need to do everything — even the mobility routine alone makes a significant difference to your recovery and next session performance.

WHY REST DAYS MATTER

Muscle fibres repair and grow during rest, not during training. Skipping rest days blunts your progress. Beginners need at least 1 rest day between every training day.

48 HRS — muscle repair window
7–9 HRS — optimal sleep target
3×/WEEK — your training frequency
MORNING
MORNING WALK
10–20 minutes of light walking outside. Increases blood flow, reduces soreness and improves mood. Low intensity only — this is not cardio.
HYDRATION
Drink 500ml of water within 30 minutes of waking. Aim for 2.5–3 litres total throughout the day. Dehydration slows recovery significantly.
PROTEIN BREAKFAST
Hit 30–40g of protein at breakfast. Eggs, Greek yogurt, cottage cheese or a protein shake. Protein synthesis stays elevated for 24–48 hrs post-training.
MOBILITY ROUTINE

10–15 minutes total. Do this in order. Hold each stretch for the time shown.

1
Hip Flexor Stretch 60 SEC EACH SIDE

Kneel on one knee, push hips forward gently. Essential if you sit for long periods.

2
Thoracic Rotation 45 SEC EACH SIDE

Sit cross-legged, place hand behind head, rotate upper back open. Improves pressing and rowing mechanics.

3
Hamstring Doorway Stretch 60 SEC EACH SIDE

Lie on your back, use a doorframe or wall to elevate one leg. Keep the leg straight.

4
Child's Pose with Reach 60 SEC

Sit back on heels, reach arms forward on the floor. Decompress the lower back and open the lats.

5
Deep Squat Hold 60 SEC

Hold a door frame for balance if needed. Work toward sitting comfortably in a full squat position over time.
