General Training Principles

Core philosophies underpinning this beginner workout block.

Effort & RPE
Higher overall efforts than standard programs. Advanced intensification techniques (myo-reps, drop sets) are used strategically — not on every set.
Joint Health 5:34
Machine work is prioritized on hypertrophy days to allow training closer to failure without overloading joints and stabilizers the way free weights do.
Eccentric Focus 3:02 6:18
Multiple exercises use a slow 3–4 second lowering phase to maximize muscle fiber disruption and growth stimulus beyond just concentric effort.
Myo-Reps 6:35
On select exercises: hit a heavy set to near-failure, rest 2–3 breaths, then do clusters of 4 reps until you can no longer hit 4 clean reps.
Tempo Monitoring 7:04 8:30
For high-rep cable work: maintain a strict 1 sec up / 1 sec down tempo. If you can't hold it, the set is over — don't grind through sloppy reps.
Neck Training 14:44
Include direct neck work 1–2 times per week for a minimum effective dose. Often overlooked but easy to add as a finisher on push days.
